What Is Convex Finance and Why Was It Built on Top of Curve?

Convex Finance is a yield optimisation protocol built specifically on top of Curve Finance. To understand Convex, you must first understand Curve's veCRV boost system: Curve's gauge emissions can be multiplied up to 2.5× by locking CRV as veCRV — but the lock is up to 4 years and non-transferable. Most LPs lack the capital commitment or time horizon to individually lock enough CRV for maximum boost.

Convex solves this by aggregating: it accepts CRV deposits from anyone, converts them to a massive shared veCRV position, and applies that collective boost to all Curve LPs who deposit through Convex. The result is that a small LP depositing through Convex earns near-maximum CRV yield without locking a single CRV token themselves.

For Curve LPs

Deposit Curve LP tokens into Convex and automatically receive near-maximum CRV gauge boost plus additional CVX rewards — no veCRV position required, no 4-year lockup, no governance complexity.

Stake CRV via Convex as cvxCRV — earn trading fees from Curve, CRV gauge rewards, and CVX tokens in a liquid position rather than a 4-year locked veCRV. Tradeable on secondary markets unlike native veCRV.

How Convex Boosts CRV Rewards: veCRV Pooling Explained

Curve's gauge boost formula rewards LPs who hold veCRV proportional to their LP position. The maximum 2.5× boost requires holding a large amount of veCRV relative to your LP size. For small-to-medium LPs, this is economically impractical — the capital needed in veCRV to max-boost a given LP position often exceeds the LP position itself.

ScenarioCRV boost receivedRequirement
No veCRV held 1× (base rate) Just being an LP — minimum possible yield
Small personal veCRV position 1.1× – 1.8× (partial boost) Lock CRV for 1–4 years, size limits boost ceiling
Maximum personal veCRV (impractical) 2.5× (full boost) Lock enormous CRV for 4 years — capital inefficient for most
Via Convex ~2.0× – 2.5× (near-max) No CRV lockup — just deposit LP tokens into Convex
Why the boost isn't always exactly 2.5×: Convex's boost across all its depositors is an average. Pools with more Convex depositors relative to the available veCRV weight receive slightly less than max boost. But for virtually all depositors, Convex delivers meaningfully more than an unboosted LP position.

cvxCRV: Liquid CRV Staking on Convex — Mechanics, Yield Sources, and the Peg

cvxCRV is a liquid wrapper for veCRV created by Convex. When you deposit CRV into Convex, you receive cvxCRV — a freely tradeable token representing your share of Convex's veCRV position, without the 4-year lockup.

cvxCRV yield sources

Staking cvxCRV earns three streams simultaneously: Curve protocol fees (3CRV), a share of CRV gauge rewards from Convex's veCRV position, and CVX token emissions from Convex's own schedule.

Curve fees (3CRV)CRV rewardsCVX emissions

The cvxCRV peg

cvxCRV should trade at or near 1 CRV, but it frequently trades at a discount because the conversion from CRV → cvxCRV is one-way (you cannot convert back). Discounts arise when selling pressure exceeds new CRV deposits. Check the Curve cvxCRV/CRV pool before converting.

One-way conversionOften at discountCheck pool first
Critical: CRV → cvxCRV is irreversible on Convex. You cannot convert cvxCRV back to CRV through Convex directly. To exit, you sell cvxCRV on the Curve cvxCRV/CRV pool — which means you may receive slightly less than 1 CRV per cvxCRV depending on current pool depth and discount. Factor this into your decision before converting.

vlCVX: Vote Locking, Gauge Voting, and Bribe Rewards Explained

vlCVX (vote-locked CVX) is obtained by locking CVX for a 16-week rolling period. It is the governance instrument that controls how Convex — which holds one of the largest veCRV positions in existence — votes on Curve's gauge weight allocations each epoch.

What vlCVX controls

vlCVX holders vote every two weeks on which Curve pools receive the largest share of CRV gauge emissions. Because Convex controls a substantial portion of total veCRV, its votes significantly affect how billions of dollars in CRV rewards are distributed across the Curve ecosystem.

Gauge weightsCRV emission directionEvery 2 weeks

The 16-week lock

CVX locked as vlCVX is illiquid for a rolling 16-week period. At the end of each 16-week cycle you can either relock or withdraw. Unlike veCRV which decays, vlCVX does not decay in weight — you retain full governance power until you unlock.

16-week rolling lockNo weight decayRelock or exit
vlCVX yield stack: By holding vlCVX you earn simultaneously — (1) bribes from protocols paying for your Curve gauge votes, (2) a share of Convex's platform fees from cvxCRV stakers, and (3) any CVX staking rewards. This multi-source yield is why vlCVX is often more capital efficient than simply staking CVX passively.

The Curve Wars: Why Protocols Fight for veCRV and Convex's Central Role

The Curve Wars is the ongoing competition between DeFi protocols to accumulate veCRV voting power — because controlling gauge votes means directing CRV emissions to your liquidity pools, reducing your protocol's cost of liquidity.

For a stablecoin protocol, DEX, or yield product, having deep Curve liquidity is a competitive necessity. CRV gauge emissions attract LPs by boosting their yield. The more gauge weight directed to your pool, the cheaper it is to bootstrap and maintain liquidity. This is why protocols spend millions acquiring veCRV — or bribing vlCVX holders to vote for them.

Bribes on Votium and Hidden Hand: How vlCVX Generates Extra Yield

Bribes are payments made by protocols to vlCVX holders in exchange for directing Convex's veCRV votes toward their specific Curve gauge. They are called "bribes" colloquially — they are fully transparent, on-chain incentive payments, not covert payments.

How Votium works

Votium is the primary bribe marketplace for Convex. Protocols deposit tokens as bribes for a given epoch. vlCVX holders who vote for a protocol's gauge via Votium claim their proportional share of the bribe after the voting period closes.

Track current bribe rates on Votium or Hidden Hand dashboards before making locking decisions based on bribe yield expectations.

How to Use Convex Finance: Depositing Curve LP Tokens Step-by-Step

  1. Obtain Curve LP tokens first — provide liquidity on Curve Finance for any supported pool (e.g. 3pool, FRAX/USDC, stETH/ETH) to receive LP tokens.
  2. Go to the official Convex Finance app — bookmark the official URL and verify the domain before connecting your wallet.
  3. Find your pool — search for the Curve pool matching your LP tokens. Convex lists all supported gauges with their current APRs broken down by CRV, CVX, and trading fee components.
  4. Approve and deposit LP tokens — approve the Convex contract to interact with your LP tokens, then confirm the deposit transaction.
  5. Monitor accruing rewards — CRV and CVX rewards accrue continuously. Check the Convex dashboard for pending reward balances.
  6. Claim rewards — claim CRV and CVX via the dashboard. You can claim directly or optionally convert CRV to cvxCRV at claim time for additional cvxCRV staking yield.
  7. Withdraw LP tokens — at any time, withdraw your LP tokens back to your wallet. There is no lockup for LP depositors on Convex.
No lockup for LP depositors: Unlike staking CRV as cvxCRV or locking CVX as vlCVX, depositing Curve LP tokens on Convex has no lockup period. You can withdraw your LP tokens at any time — only the gas cost of the withdrawal transaction applies.

Best Practices for Convex Depositors and vlCVX Holders

  • Check the cvxCRV/CRV pool discount before converting CRV — if cvxCRV trades at a significant discount, it may be better to buy cvxCRV on the secondary market rather than convert at 1:1.
  • Understand the full yield breakdown — Convex APRs consist of CRV (variable), CVX (variable), and trading fees (variable). All three components fluctuate — a headline APR from yesterday may differ significantly from today's.
  • For vlCVX: time your lock around the 16-week cycle and vote every epoch via Votium to capture maximum bribe yield. Unclaimed bribe epochs are forfeited if you miss the claim window.
  • Never lock vlCVX with capital needed in under 16 weeks — the lock is strict and there is no emergency exit mechanism.
  • Revoke stale token approvals after withdrawing from Convex — the multi-contract architecture means multiple approvals accumulate. Use Revoke.cash periodically.
  • Bookmark the official Convex URL and verify the domain every session — DeFi phishing sites frequently target high-TVL protocols like Convex.
  • Consider Curve pool underlying asset risk — the yield from Convex is only as good as the safety of the underlying Curve pool. A depeg in a stablecoin pool's assets affects your LP value regardless of the CRV boost.

Troubleshooting Convex Finance: Rewards, cvxCRV Peg, and vlCVX Expiry

"My CRV/CVX rewards are lower than the displayed APR"

  • Displayed APR is a snapshot calculated at current token prices and emission rates — both fluctuate constantly. Realised yield over time reflects average prices and rates during your deposit period.
  • Ensure your LP tokens are actually deposited and staked in Convex — deposits that weren't confirmed due to gas failures will not generate rewards.

"cvxCRV is trading well below 1 CRV — should I be worried?"

  • A discount on cvxCRV is structurally expected due to the one-way conversion — it is a known feature, not a bug or exploit signal. Historical discounts have ranged from ~1% to ~20% depending on market conditions.
  • If the discount is very large (>15%), it may signal broader market stress or reduced demand for CRV yield — monitor Curve governance and market conditions before acting.
  • A large discount also creates a buying opportunity for cvxCRV if you intend to stake it long-term — buying at discount improves your effective entry yield.

"My vlCVX lock has expired but I can't see my CVX"

  • Expired vlCVX does not automatically return to your wallet — you must manually withdraw via the Convex governance page after the lock period ends.
  • If you don't withdraw before the next relock window, your expired vlCVX may be automatically relocked — check the current auto-relock settings in your account.
Always verify on the official Convex app: Third-party dashboards and aggregators may show stale or incorrect APR data. For current reward accrual, pending claims, and lock status — the official Convex Finance app is the authoritative interface.
